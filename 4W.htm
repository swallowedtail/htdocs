PRIVATE LAMB V.C. (Victoria Cross Medal)

(awarded for capturing a German Machine Gun Post despite being shot in the leg 3 times). Private Lamb returned to the front after 2 months recovery in England. Private Lamb was killed by a Sniper on Christmas Day 1918 aged 19.

 

WAITING TO GO OVER THE TOP -  FRANCE 1916

 

A BRITISH SNIPER IN HIS TRENCH

 

 

 

7.30AM JULY 1ST 1916. TROOPS OF THE ESSEX REGIMENT GO OVER THE TOP. AT THE END OF THE DAY 20,000 BRITISH TROOPS HAD BEEN KILLED AND 40,000 WOUNDED.

Left to right: Corporal H. Ansell (killed in Action), Private C. Abbott (killed in Action), Lance Corporal E. Street (Died of wounds), Sergeant E. Stalker (wounded in action, lost both legs), Second Lieutenant H. Barton (survived the battle but was gassed at Passchendael in 1918), Private J. Coleman (Killed in Action), Lance Corporal A Turnbull (boy soldier aged 16 – Killed in Action – no known grave), Private L. Powell (survived the war but was young enough to serve in the Second World War. He died at Dunkirk in 1940)
